## Purpose

New shared helper that emergency-flattens + DB-closes an ALREADY-tracked open position — the T-534b2 SL-watchdog's exact need: a position that exists on Bybit AND in `position_state` whose exchange-side SL has vanished. T-530/T-534a-shaped **no-caller** leaf — T-534b2 (watchdog tick, blocked-by this) is the first consumer.

## Why a NEW helper (OQ-A=A; WG#1)

`placement_persist.emergency_close` (:176-189) requires placement-time artifacts the periodic watchdog does NOT have: `request: OrderRequest`, `envelope: MessageEnvelope`, `place_result: OrderPlaceResult`, `fill_price`. It also `insert_order`×2 + creates a NEW trade (SL-set-failure scenario, trade not yet persisted). The watchdog's position is ALREADY fully persisted (existing `trade_id` in `position_state`). Reusing `emergency_close` would force synthetic reconstruction of OrderRequest/envelope/place_result (anti-pattern). **TASKS.md T-534b2-line text "reuse `placement_persist.emergency_close`" is deliberately NOT followed** — operator-approved OQ-A=A; the architecturally correct precedent is `restart.py:_close_orphan_db` (existing-trade close: resolve close_order_id from `trade_id`, tx-wrap `update_trade_close` + `delete_position_state`). WG#1 records this so brief-reviewer does not flag the TASKS.md divergence as scope-drift.
